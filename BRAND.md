# Brand — voice, audience, personality

> **Template skeleton.** Fill every section with this project's brand. This is
> the "why" behind every visual and content decision. `DESIGN.md` (the "how")
> must serve what is described here — if a token or pattern does not, it is wrong
> and should be reworked.

## One-sentence brand mood

> _3–5 adjectives capturing the feeling. Example: "Warm, calm, grounded.
> Personal not corporate."_

## Who it is

_What the brand/site is, who is behind it, and what it sells or offers._

## Audience

_Who the site is for: demographics, mindset, what they want, how they should feel._

## Tone of voice

- _Person and formality (e.g. intimate second-person vs. formal)._
- _Attitude (reassuring vs. authoritative, playful vs. serious)._
- _Vocabulary and recurring themes — the brand's belief system._

## Visual personality

_High-level visual feel (not tokens): color temperature, shapes, imagery style,
recurring motifs. Tokens live in `DESIGN.md`._

## Emotional positioning

- _Where the brand sits on key axes (e.g. peer vs. authority, playful vs. mature)._
- _What it is **not**._

## How to use this document

When making any design or content decision, sanity-check it against the
descriptions above. This file is the **tiebreaker** when other documents leave
room for interpretation. Agents doing UI work should read this file first, then
`DESIGN.md`.
